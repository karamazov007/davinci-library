Preparation — The 3 Layers

Champions often practice harder than they play — they're inoculating themselves against surprise.

1

Technical Mastery

  • Drill until automatic
  • Stored in procedural memory, not conscious thought
  • Thousands of reps of the same skill

When the conscious brain is hijacked by anxiety, the body still executes what's deeply encoded.

2

Scenario Rehearsal

  • Pressure training — points that "count"
  • Visualization of self & opponent
  • Varied contexts: noise, crowds, fatigue

Simulate pressure before the real thing arrives.

3

Routine & Anchoring

  • Breathing patterns
  • Physical rituals (ball bounce, free-throw routine)
  • Mental cues — "trust the process"

Attention anchors to reset focus when pressure builds.

02 /
The Anxiety Reframe

Top performers don't eliminate pre-match nerves — that's normal and universal. They reinterpret them.

Anxious reading
"I'm scared,
I might fail"
→ scattered, paralyzing
→
Excited reading
"My body is energized,
I'm ready"
→ channeled, performance-boosting

// same heart-racing physiology — different interpretation

Elite performers aren't relaxed before matches — they're alert, focused & energized. That energy is channeled through preparation, not scattered.
03 /
Handling External Pressure

Stronger opponent. Loud crowd. Bad luck. Three techniques keep these from hijacking focus.

1

Locus of Control — process > outcome

✓ Control
  • Your preparation
  • Your effort & focus
  • Your in-the-moment decisions
✕ Don't control
  • Opponent's skill
  • Crowd reactions
  • Luck

Worrying whether you'll win is paralyzing. Executing your game plan is actionable.

2

Point-by-Point Partition

Break the match into micro-units. Play one point at a time, reset after each, and don't look at the scoreboard mentally. "I'm down against a better team" becomes "What's the next thing I need to execute?"

3

Prepared Contingencies

Already thought through before the match: "If I'm down 0–2, here's what I do… if they play an unexpected style, my adjustment is…" So when pressure hits, you're not panicked — you're executing a rehearsed Plan B.

Attention Filtering

Under pressure the brain is bombarded — crowd, doubts, opponent, score. Athletes practice selective attention, tuning everything out except the immediate task.

Ball + breath · target line + swing feel · stroke rhythm

Neutral State Between Plays

No big celebration after a good shot (spikes emotion). No spiral after a bad one (destabilizes focus). A neutral, business-like demeanor — then reset.

Even keel = stable focus
05 /
The Team Component

In team sports the pressure is distributed — but also more complicated.

The trade-offs

+Accountable to others — motivation boost
–Dependent on others — loss of control
~Momentum is collective — can cascade

What teams use

▸Collective routines — rituals, huddles
▸Role clarity — each player's responsibility
▸Communication — constant feedback

During Match // manage the moment
├──Reframe anxiety as readiness
├──Focus on process, not outcome
├──Use micro-routines between plays
├──Filter attention to what matters

├──Reset using routine
├──Don't spiral into self-doubt
