The Stamina
Blueprint

Stamina is not one thing. It is three distinct energy systems — and they have almost nothing to do with each other in terms of how you train them. Getting this wrong is why most people's stamina never meaningfully improves.

// the reframe that changes the entire training approach
You are not unfit. You have an underdeveloped aerobic base — and that is an entirely different problem with an entirely different fix.

The aerobic base is trainable, predictable, and responds reliably to the right stimulus. The timeline is weeks, not years. But only if you train the right system in the right way — which almost nobody does by default.

The Four Pillars of Stamina

Beyond the energy systems — what your body actually needs to sustain effort.
❤️

Cardiac Output

How much blood your heart pumps per beat. A trained heart's left ventricle is physically larger and pumps more per beat — so it doesn't need to beat as fast to deliver the same oxygen. Untrained: 70-80 bpm resting. Trained: 45-55 bpm. During exercise, the trained heart sustains output at lower rates, delaying exhaustion.

built by → sustained aerobic training over months
🫁

Lung Efficiency

How effectively your lungs extract oxygen and expel CO₂. Less trainable than the heart but improves meaningfully with sustained aerobic work. Becomes a limiting factor less often than people assume — the heart and muscles are usually the bottleneck first.

built by → aerobic training, breathing practice
⚡

Mitochondrial Density

Mitochondria are the engines inside each muscle cell that convert oxygen into usable energy. More mitochondria = more aerobic capacity at the cellular level. This is why Zone 2 training works — it directly stimulates mitochondrial growth inside the working muscles.

built by → sustained moderate-intensity training over months
🔥

Lactate Threshold

The intensity level at which your body switches from primarily aerobic to primarily anaerobic. The higher your threshold, the harder you can work before the burning and heaviness set in. This is the most directly trainable stamina metric for sports performance.

built by → threshold training — the uncomfortable-but-sustainable zone

// the zone 2 test

Can you hold a conversation? Yes but slightly breathless = Zone 2. Can talk easily = too easy (Zone 1). Can only say 2-3 words = too hard (Zone 3+). Heart rate roughly 120-140 bpm for most people.

⚠️
// the most common mistake — kills progress

Always training too hard. Most beginners push into exhaustion every session. This trains System 2 but neglects the aerobic base entirely. Elite endurance athletes spend 70-80% of their training time in Zone 2. It feels too easy. That is the point.

STRATEGY 2Raise the Lactate Thresholdadd after 4-6 weeks of base

Once you have 4-6 weeks of aerobic base, add threshold training. Sustained effort at the intensity just below where the burning starts — uncomfortable but sustainable for 10-20 minutes at a stretch.

This directly pushes your lactate threshold upward, meaning you can play at higher intensities before the heaviness sets in. The effort level: you can say a few words but cannot hold a conversation. 2-3 repetitions of 8 minutes with 3 minutes easy walking between each.

STRATEGY 3Train Sport-Specific Intervalsadd at week 9+ only

Badminton, pickleball, tennis — all interval sports. 5-15 seconds of high intensity, brief recovery, repeat. Your training should mirror this. But only once your aerobic base can support it — otherwise you just accumulate oxygen debt every rep.

Shuttle runs, shadow footwork, jumping jacks — anything that mirrors real sport movement. 10-15 repetitions. Trains System 2 capacity and trains your body to recover faster between explosive efforts.

STRATEGY 4Reduce the Oxygen Cost of Movementthe underrated one

A significant part of why you exhaust quickly is movement inefficiency. You are spending more energy than necessary to cover the court because your footwork and positioning are not yet economical. As technique improves, the same distance costs less energy.

This is why experienced players can play for 2 hours looking relaxed while beginners are exhausted in 20 minutes. They have become metabolically efficient at the movements. Better technique is literally a stamina upgrade — no cardiovascular training required.

The Two Accelerators

Separate from training — but they determine how fast everything above works.
⚖️

Body Composition

Carrying excess body weight directly increases the oxygen cost of every movement. Every kilogram of additional mass your heart has to oxygenate and your legs have to carry is a direct tax on your stamina. This is not about aesthetics — it is mechanics.

Your InBody scans already give you this data. The correlation between fat mass reduction and stamina improvement is direct and measurable. Same training, lower mass = meaningfully better stamina numbers.

😴

Sleep

Your aerobic adaptations — mitochondrial growth, cardiac remodelling — happen during sleep, not during training. Training is the stimulus. Sleep is when the body actually builds the thing you trained for.

7-8 hours accelerates stamina gains faster than almost any other variable. Consistently sleeping 6 hours or less cuts your adaptation rate roughly in half — the same sessions produce half the physiological improvement.
