LAYER1
Principles — the invariants
5–8 fundamentals per domain that don't change across variations.
🏋️

Workout

8 invariants
  1. Scapular — shoulders back & down
  2. Spinal — neutral, no over-curve
  3. Load path — weight in muscle, not joints
  4. ROM — full stretch to contraction
  5. Breathing — exhale on exertion
  6. Mind-muscle — feel the target
  7. Progression — small increments
  8. Rest — consistent between sets
🏊

Swimming

7 invariants
  1. Head — neutral, waterline at hairline
  2. Rotation — hip + shoulder as a unit
  3. Recovery — hand height/angle on entry
  4. Breath — part of rotation, not a stop
  5. Kick sync — timed to arm cycle
  6. Core — flat body, hips up
  7. Catch — engage water right after entry
🥗

Nutrition

6 invariants
  1. Timing — pre / intra / post differ
  2. Digestive load — stomach work vs. energy
  3. Hydration — affects all processing
  4. Macros — carb/protein/fat balance
  5. Tolerance — what your gut handles
  6. Availability — when fuel kicks in
LAYER2
Protocol — the four stages
Every activity runs the same Pre → During → Between → Post structure.
~15 min before

Pre

  • Fuel & hydration — what, when, how it felt
  • Warm up
  • Set intention: one principle today
  • Write your single cue
within set / repeat

During

  • Which principle is being tested?
  • Target engaged or compensating?
  • First sign form breaks
  • One thing working / breaking
60–90s rest

Between

  • What changed from last set?
  • One variable to adjust next
✍ WRITE IT DOWN — 2–3 words
~5 min after

Post

  • Stretch + plan nutrition
  • Rate energy & form 1–10
  • One key insight
  • One hypothesis for next time

The same skeleton flexes to workout, swim or nutrition — only the principle being tested changes.

// the real point
Your framework isn't the problem.
Your capture discipline is.

You already know the principles — you proved it by rattling them off. The gap isn't understanding. It's the 2-minute investment between sets to write down what's actually happening vs. what should be.

"barbell on traps, not neck" "shoulders back & down" "breath syncs with rotation"
Two weeks. One domain. One principle.

That's enough to see exactly where the leakage is happening — then the loop does the rest.
