Under the Hood — Same Move Every Time

Each lens finds something interesting, then asks: "now where can I go with it?" Without expansion, the joke dies on the table.
Ironymarriage counselor gets divorced
without expansion

"Marriage counselor got divorced."

…end. nothing happens.
with expansion
Marriage→Advice→Expertise→Failure→Teaching failure
"Come learn what not to do."
Character Humoran extremely arrogant character
without expansion

"He's arrogant."

…the character alone isn't the joke.
with expansion
How he sees himself→How reality sees him→What situations reveal it→What consequences follow

without expansion

"He's lazy."

…flat.
with expansion (→ extreme)

The One Exception

🔤 Wordplay leans on language, not association

Not every humor skill relies equally on expansion. Wordplay draws more on:

vocabularyphoneticsdouble meaningslanguage patterns
"Time flies like an arrow; fruit flies like a banana."

That's linguistic, not primarily associative.

But even here — after you find the double meaning, you usually use associations to expand it into a full bit.

Before studying every lens —
spend a month on expansion alone.

Improvements there raise your game across irony, character, hyperbole, absurdism, sitcom writing, and observational comedy simultaneously. The gap between one or two directions and ten, twenty, fifty is the gap between an interesting observation and an actual joke.

interesting observation  +  many directions  =  actual joke
Sharpen the drill all you want — without power it doesn't cut.

Observation finds it, association multiplies it, taste picks the winner. Train the middle one and everything downstream gets easier.
